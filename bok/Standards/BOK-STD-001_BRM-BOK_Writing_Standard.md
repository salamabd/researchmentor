# 1. Executive Summary

This standard defines the editorial, structural, and documentation conventions used throughout the Business Research Mentoring Body of Knowledge (BRM-BOK).

Its purpose is to ensure that every canonical document is consistent, traceable, professionally structured, and suitable for long-term governance.

This standard governs document presentation. It does not govern educational content.

---

# 2. Purpose

The Writing Standard establishes a uniform editorial framework that enables:

* consistency across the Body of Knowledge;
* long-term maintainability;
* reliable traceability;
* simplified review;
* publication-quality documentation.

All canonical documents shall conform to this standard unless an approved exception exists.

---

# 3. Scope

This standard applies to:

* Charters
* Framework documents
* Educational theories
* Methodologies
* Reference architectures
* Specifications
* Governance documents
* Standards
* Registers
* Validation reports

Working notes, journals, and temporary design discussions are excluded.

---

# 4. Document Classification

BRM-BOK recognizes three classes of documents.

## Canonical

Normative documents forming part of BRM-BOK.

Examples:

* Charter
* BRMF
* BRMM
* REA
* Standards

## Working

Supporting documents under active development.

Examples:

* design studies
* comparisons
* research notes
* literature maps

## Temporary

Personal notes, experiments, brainstorming material, and disposable drafts.

Temporary documents shall never be cited as canonical references.

---

# 5. Mandatory Metadata

Every canonical document shall begin with a metadata section containing at least:

* Document ID
* Canonical Name
* Body of Knowledge
* Volume (if applicable)
* Part (if applicable)
* Version
* Status
* Classification
* Normative Status
* Repository Path
* Parent Documents
* Dependent Documents
* Review Cycle

Additional metadata may be included when appropriate.

---

# 6. Standard Document Structure

Canonical documents should follow the sequence below where applicable:

1. Executive Summary
2. Purpose
3. Scope
4. Definitions
5. Normative Statements
6. Informative Guidance
7. Educational Rationale
8. Platform Implications
9. Governance Requirements
10. Acceptance Criteria
11. Cross References
12. Revision History

Sections that are not applicable may be omitted with justification.

---

# 7. Normative Language

The following terms have specific meanings.

**MUST** — mandatory requirement.

**MUST NOT** — prohibited requirement.

**SHOULD** — recommended practice.

**SHOULD NOT** — generally discouraged.

**MAY** — optional implementation.

These terms shall be interpreted consistently throughout BRM-BOK.

---

# 8. Writing Principles

Canonical documents shall be:

* clear;
* concise;
* precise;
* technology-independent where possible;
* educationally focused;
* internally consistent;
* free from marketing language;
* written in professional academic English.

The Body of Knowledge shall favour timeless principles over implementation-specific details.

---

# 9. Terminology

Canonical terminology shall conform to the BRM Lexicon.

Before introducing a new term, authors shall determine whether an equivalent canonical term already exists.

New canonical terminology requires governance review.

---

# 10. Traceability

Every canonical document shall identify:

* its parent document(s);
* dependent document(s);
* related theories;
* related methodologies;
* related architectures.

Normative requirements should be traceable throughout the Body of Knowledge.

---

# 11. Figures and Diagrams

Diagrams shall:

* have a unique title;
* include a figure number where appropriate;
* support the surrounding text;
* avoid contradicting normative statements.

Text remains authoritative where conflicts arise.

---

# 12. Tables

Tables should:

* include a descriptive title;
* define abbreviations where necessary;
* remain understandable independently of surrounding text.

---

# 13. Cross References

Cross references should use canonical identifiers.

Example:

BRMF-003 — Educational Philosophy

rather than informal descriptions.

---

# 14. Revision History

Each canonical document shall maintain a revision history including:

* version;
* date;
* summary of changes;
* approval status.

Earlier revisions shall remain traceable.

---

# 15. Acceptance Criteria

A canonical document is considered complete when it:

* conforms to this writing standard;
* uses approved terminology;
* contains required metadata;
* identifies traceability relationships;
* satisfies its educational objective;
* has undergone governance review.

---

# 16. Compliance

All future canonical BRM-BOK documents shall comply with this standard unless a documented exception has been approved through the established governance process.
