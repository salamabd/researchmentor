# 1. Executive Summary

The Business Research Initiative (BRI) is the governing initiative responsible for establishing, maintaining, validating, and evolving the Business Research Mentoring Body of Knowledge (BRM-BOK).

The initiative exists to improve business research education through educational science, structured mentoring, responsible artificial intelligence, knowledge engineering, and evidence-informed practice.

The initiative is independent of any particular software implementation, programming language, artificial intelligence model, or commercial product.

Software developed under the initiative is an implementation of the Body of Knowledge, not the Body of Knowledge itself.

---

# 2. Purpose

The purpose of the Business Research Initiative is to advance the theory and practice of AI-assisted business research mentoring while preserving the principles of academic integrity, scholarly independence, and human supervision.

The initiative seeks to produce educational frameworks, mentoring methodologies, reference architectures, knowledge models, software platforms, and scholarly outputs that collectively improve the development of business researchers.

---

# 3. Mission

The mission of the Business Research Initiative is:

> To improve business research education by developing governed, explainable, evidence-informed educational frameworks and technologies that strengthen research capability while preserving the central role of students, supervisors, and universities.

---

# 4. Vision

The Initiative envisions a future in which every business research student has access to structured mentoring that develops the capability to think, reason, justify, reflect, and contribute as an independent researcher.

Technology should strengthen human scholarship rather than replace it.

Educational quality should be measured by researcher development rather than document production.

---

# 5. Scope

The Initiative governs:

* the Business Research Mentoring Body of Knowledge (BRM-BOK);
* educational frameworks;
* educational theories;
* mentoring methodologies;
* educational architectures;
* knowledge engineering;
* reference models;
* software implementations derived from BRM-BOK;
* validation activities;
* scholarly dissemination.

The Initiative does not govern institutional academic regulations, assessment policies, or degree requirements, except where explicitly adopted by participating institutions.

---

# 6. Constitutional Principles

The Initiative adopts the following constitutional principles.

1. Education before automation.
2. Research capability before document production.
3. Decisions before answers.
4. Mentoring before generation.
5. Evidence before conclusions.
6. Reflection before progression.
7. Supervisor partnership before AI autonomy.
8. Explainability before convenience.
9. Governance before deployment.
10. Continuous improvement through educational evidence.

These principles are normative and shall guide all future development.

---

# 7. Governance Hierarchy

Authority within BRM-BOK shall flow through the following hierarchy:

1. Business Research Initiative Charter
2. Business Research Mentoring Framework (BRMF)
3. Educational Theories
4. Business Research Mentoring Methodology (BRMM)
5. Research Education Architecture (REA)
6. Product and Technical Specifications
7. Software Implementations
8. Validation and Evidence

Lower layers shall remain consistent with higher layers.

---

# 8. Governance Rules

The Initiative establishes the following permanent governance rules.

## Rule 1 — Knowledge Before Software

Educational knowledge shall be defined before implementation.

## Rule 2 — Complete Traceability

Every canonical artifact shall be traceable through an unbroken chain of governance.

## Rule 3 — Evidence Before Evolution

No major concept shall become canonical without appropriate educational justification and supporting evidence.

## Rule 4 — Human Authority

Students remain responsible for scholarship.

Supervisors remain responsible for academic judgement.

AI remains an educational mentor.

---

# 9. Strategic Objectives

The Initiative shall:

* establish BRM-BOK;
* develop and maintain BRMF;
* develop BRMM;
* establish and refine educational theories where justified;
* create reference architectures;
* promote responsible AI in research education;
* support institutional adoption;
* encourage scholarly publication;
* evaluate educational effectiveness through evidence.

---

# 10. Expected Outputs

The Initiative may produce:

* Bodies of Knowledge
* Frameworks
* Methodologies
* Reference Architectures
* Educational Models
* Knowledge Graphs
* Software Platforms
* Teaching Resources
* Academic Publications
* Validation Studies

---

# 11. Success Criteria

The Initiative will regard itself as successful when:

* researchers demonstrate stronger research capability;
* supervisors report improved mentoring effectiveness;
* universities adopt components of BRM-BOK;
* educational outcomes improve through evidence;
* BRM-BOK becomes a trusted reference for AI-assisted business research mentoring.

---

# 12. Normative Statements

Within BRM-BOK:

* Canonical documents **MUST** conform to governance requirements.
* Educational behavior **MUST** be explainable.
* AI **MUST NOT** replace academic judgement.
* New concepts **MUST** identify the educational problem they solve.
* Every canonical artifact **MUST** identify its dependencies and traceability.

---

# 13. Long-Term Commitment

The Initiative commits to evolving BRM-BOK through scholarship, validation, institutional collaboration, and continuous review.

Changes shall be governed through transparent processes that preserve the coherence and integrity of the Body of Knowledge.

---

# 14. Acceptance Criteria

This Charter is considered complete when it:

* establishes the identity of the Initiative;
* defines its purpose and mission;
* establishes governance authority;
* defines constitutional principles;
* provides the basis for all subsequent canonical documents.
