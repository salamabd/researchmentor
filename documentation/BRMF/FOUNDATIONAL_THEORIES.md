# Purpose

This document defines the original theoretical foundations upon which the Business Research Mentor (BRM) platform is built.

These theories govern the educational philosophy, AI mentoring behaviour, platform architecture, and future evolution of BRM.

Software implementation shall remain subordinate to these educational principles.

# Theory 1 – Decision-Centred Research Learning (DCRL)

Researchers become better researchers by making progressively better research decisions.

Research learning is therefore centred on reasoning, justification, evidence evaluation, and reflection rather than document production.

Educational implication:
- Every learning activity should improve decision quality.
- Every milestone should represent improved judgement rather than merely completed writing.

---

# Theory 2 – Research Decision Intelligence (RDI)

The fundamental unit of BRM is the research decision.

Each important decision should be explainable, evidence-informed, connected to related decisions, reviewable by supervisors, and capable of revision.

Educational implication:
- Reasoning is preserved, not just outcomes.

---

# Theory 3 – Research Capability Model (RCM)

Student development is measured through research capability rather than dissertation progress.

Illustrative capability domains:
- Problem framing
- Literature reasoning
- Theory selection
- Variable reasoning
- Methodological judgement
- Critical reflection
- Research integrity
- Scholarly independence

Educational implication:
- Capability growth is the primary indicator of success.

---

# Theory 4 – Research Decision Lifecycle (RDL)

Every significant research decision follows a common lifecycle:

Recognise
→ Explore
→ Compare
→ Justify
→ Decide
→ Review
→ Revise
→ Approve

Educational implication:
- All BRM modules should implement this lifecycle consistently.

---

# Theory 5 – Guided Cognitive Progression (GCP)

Students should only be challenged with research decisions appropriate to their current level and stage.

Prerequisite reasoning should be established before introducing later-stage complexity.

Educational implication:
- The platform progressively reveals complexity and reduces unnecessary cognitive overload.

---

# Relationship Among Theories

DCRL explains why BRM exists.
RDI explains what BRM captures.
RCM explains what BRM develops.
RDL explains how decisions evolve.
GCP explains how learning is sequenced.

Together they form the BRM Educational Architecture.

---

# Feature Evaluation Rule

Every proposed feature must answer YES to all of the following:

1. Does it improve research decision quality?
2. Does it strengthen at least one research capability?
3. Does it preserve academic integrity?
4. Does it strengthen the student–supervisor relationship?
5. Is its educational value explainable?
6. Does it align with the BRMF educational architecture?

Failure of any criterion requires redesign or rejection.
