# BRMF-102 — Scholarly Development Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

# Purpose

This document defines how BRM models the progressive development of an
independent researcher. The focus is the evolution of the learner rather than
completion of research documents.

# Fundamental Principle

Research education develops scholarly capability. Documents, presentations and
publications are evidence of that development.

# Development Dimensions

BRM evaluates progress across multiple dimensions rather than a single score.

1. Research Understanding
2. Critical Thinking
3. Scholarly Judgement
4. Methodological Competence
5. Reflective Practice
6. Research Independence

# Development Levels

Level 1 — Guided
- Requires extensive supervision.
- Identifies problems with support.

Level 2 — Emerging
- Begins making justified research decisions.
- Recognises alternatives and limitations.

Level 3 — Developing
- Integrates evidence into coherent reasoning.
- Explains methodological choices.

Level 4 — Independent
- Demonstrates sound scholarly judgement.
- Critically evaluates competing viewpoints.

Level 5 — Scholarly Leadership
- Produces original contributions.
- Mentors others and reflects on research practice.

# Role of BRM

BRM continually estimates the learner's current developmental state and selects
mentoring interventions that support progression to the next level.

# Educational Outcome

Success is measured by growth in scholarly capability rather than completion of
individual research tasks.
