# BRMF-103 — Research Judgement Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

# Purpose

This document defines Research Judgement as a central capability developed by
Business Research Mentor (BRM). Research judgement is the ability to make,
justify, evaluate and refine research decisions using evidence, reasoning and
reflection.

# Principles

Research judgement:
- develops progressively;
- is context dependent;
- requires justification;
- improves through feedback;
- remains open to revision.

# Components

1. Problem Framing
2. Evidence Evaluation
3. Alternative Comparison
4. Decision Justification
5. Reflection and Revision

# Observable Indicators

Learners increasingly:
- identify assumptions;
- recognise uncertainty;
- compare competing explanations;
- justify methodological choices;
- revise conclusions when warranted.

# Mentoring Implications

BRM should:
- ask questions before giving answers;
- request justification;
- encourage alternative viewpoints;
- prompt reflective review;
- explain recommendations.

# Assessment

Judgement is assessed through patterns of reasoning rather than by a single
correct answer.

# Relationship to BRMF-102

Research judgement is one dimension of overall scholarly development and
contributes to increasing research independence.

End of BRMF-103.
