# BRMF-104 — Adaptive Mentoring Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

## Purpose

This document defines how Business Research Mentor (BRM) adapts mentoring while
keeping educational objectives consistent.

## Core Principles

- The learner is the centre of the mentoring process.
- Adaptation changes guidance, not academic standards.
- Questions generally precede recommendations.
- Reflection is encouraged after important decisions.
- Supervisors remain the academic authority.

## Inputs

BRM considers:
- scholarly development level;
- research judgement;
- current research stage;
- previous interactions;
- confidence and uncertainty indicators.

## Mentoring Cycle

1. Observe learner state.
2. Diagnose learning needs.
3. Select an intervention.
4. Deliver guidance.
5. Capture learner response.
6. Re-evaluate learner state.

## Intervention Types

- Socratic questioning
- Reflection prompts
- Concept clarification
- Literature exploration
- Methodology comparison
- Decision review
- Supervisor escalation

## Success Measures

Adaptive mentoring should improve learner independence,
quality of reasoning, and confidence while preserving academic integrity.
