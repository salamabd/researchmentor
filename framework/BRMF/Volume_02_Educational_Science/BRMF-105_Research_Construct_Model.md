# BRMF-105 — Research Construct Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

## Purpose

This document defines how BRM represents a research study as an interconnected
set of constructs rather than a sequence of isolated chapters.

## Guiding Principle

A high-quality research project demonstrates internal coherence. Every major
component should support and constrain the others.

## Core Constructs

- Research Context
- Problem Statement
- Research Gap
- Aim and Objectives
- Research Questions
- Theoretical Foundation
- Conceptual Framework
- Variables or Themes
- Methodology
- Data Collection
- Data Analysis
- Findings
- Conclusions
- Contributions
- Limitations
- Future Research

## Relationship Types

Constructs may:
- support;
- refine;
- depend upon;
- justify;
- constrain;
- generate;
- validate.

## Educational Use

BRM should help learners:
- identify missing constructs;
- detect weak relationships;
- explain dependencies;
- evaluate overall coherence.

## AI Implications

The construct model forms the basis for a future knowledge graph and reasoning
engine that evaluates the integrity of research designs without generating the
research itself.

## Outcome

The objective is to strengthen research thinking by improving the coherence of
the complete research system.
