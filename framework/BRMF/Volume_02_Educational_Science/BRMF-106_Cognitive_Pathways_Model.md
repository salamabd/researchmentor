# BRMF-106 — Cognitive Pathways Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

## Purpose

This document defines how Business Research Mentor (BRM) models the different
ways learners think and progress through the research process. It recognises
that successful researchers may follow different cognitive pathways while
working toward the same scholarly standards.

## Educational Principle

There is no single correct path to becoming a capable researcher. Diversity in
thinking is compatible with consistency in academic quality.

## Cognitive Pathway Dimensions

BRM recognises variation in:

- Problem exploration
- Information seeking
- Concept formation
- Critical evaluation
- Decision making
- Reflection
- Knowledge integration

## Illustrative Pathways

### Exploratory Pathway
Learners refine understanding through broad investigation before narrowing
their focus.

### Hypothesis-Driven Pathway
Learners begin with an explicit proposition and evaluate it through systematic
evidence.

### Iterative Pathway
Learners repeatedly revisit earlier decisions as understanding develops.

### Structured Pathway
Learners prefer a planned sequence of well-defined research stages.

## Adaptive Mentoring

BRM should identify the learner's current pathway and tailor mentoring without
lowering academic expectations. Adaptation affects guidance, not standards.

## Indicators

Potential indicators include:
- frequency of revision;
- diversity of sources consulted;
- justification quality;
- responsiveness to feedback;
- confidence under uncertainty.
