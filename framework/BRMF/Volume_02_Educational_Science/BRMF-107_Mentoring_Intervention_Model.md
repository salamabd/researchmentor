# BRMF-107 — Mentoring Intervention Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

## Purpose

This document defines the intervention framework used by Business Research
Mentor (BRM). An intervention is a deliberate educational action selected to
advance the learner's scholarly development rather than simply solve an
immediate research problem.

## Guiding Principles

- Every intervention has an educational purpose.
- Guidance should strengthen independent thinking.
- Interventions should be explainable and evidence-informed.
- Escalate to the supervisor when appropriate.

## Intervention Categories

1. Socratic Questioning
2. Concept Clarification
3. Literature Exploration
4. Critical Comparison
5. Methodology Guidance
6. Research Design Review
7. Reflective Prompting
8. Progress Planning
9. Supervisor Escalation

## Selection Factors

BRM selects interventions based on:
- scholarly development level;
- research judgement;
- cognitive pathway;
- current research stage;
- confidence and uncertainty;
- prior intervention history.

## Intervention Cycle

Observe → Diagnose → Select → Deliver → Reflect → Evaluate → Adapt

## Explainability

Each intervention should record:
- why it was selected;
- learner state;
- expected educational outcome;
- follow-up indicators.

## Expected Outcomes

Effective interventions should improve:
- reasoning quality;
- research independence;
- reflective practice;
- decision justification;
- confidence without reducing academic standards.
