# BRMF-109 — Reflection and Feedback Model

Status: Draft 1.0
Classification: Educational Science

## Purpose

This document defines how Business Research Mentor (BRM) uses reflection and
feedback to strengthen scholarly capability. Reflection transforms experience
into learning, while feedback provides evidence that supports improvement.

## Educational Principles

- Reflection is an intentional learning activity.
- Feedback should promote understanding rather than dependency.
- Constructive feedback is specific, timely, and actionable.
- Reflection and feedback form a continuous improvement cycle.

## Reflection Cycle

1. Experience
2. Describe
3. Analyse
4. Evaluate
5. Plan
6. Apply

## Feedback Sources

- Self-reflection
- AI mentoring
- Academic supervisor
- Peer discussion
- Assessment outcomes

## Feedback Categories

- Conceptual understanding
- Research judgement
- Methodological reasoning
- Critical evaluation
- Scholarly communication
- Research planning

## Educational Use

BRM should:
- encourage structured reflection after significant decisions;
- explain feedback using educational evidence;
- identify recurring learning patterns;
- connect feedback to future mentoring interventions.

## Integration with Learner State

Reflection and feedback may update:
- scholarly development level;
- research judgement profile;
- confidence indicators;
- growth opportunities;
- intervention priorities.

## Explainability

Every important recommendation should identify:
- the reflection or feedback considered;
- its educational significance;
- the expected learning outcome.
